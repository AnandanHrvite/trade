1. Strategy Overview

The PRICE_ACTION_5M strategy is a 5-minute timeframe system designed for NIFTY index options (CE/PE). Unlike indicator-heavy approaches, it reads what price itself is doing — identifying candlestick patterns and chart patterns at key Support/Resistance levels, confirmed by RSI momentum.

Core Principle

Price action trading focuses on price structure rather than lagging indicators. The strategy identifies 9 pattern types, but only takes trades when the pattern occurs at a meaningful S/R level (swing high/low). RSI acts as a lightweight confirmation filter — not the primary signal.

Why Price Action on 5-Min?

  • More opportunities than 15-min (3x candles per day)
  • Cleaner patterns than 3-min (less noise, fewer false signals)
  • Reasonable SL from signal candle wick (typically 10-20 pts)
  • No lag — reacts to what price is doing NOW, not what indicators say it was doing

2. Support & Resistance Detection

S/R levels are derived dynamically from swing highs and swing lows in the last 30 candles. A swing high is a candle whose high is greater than both neighbors. A swing low is a candle whose low is less than both neighbors.

3. Entry Patterns — Candlestick

Nine patterns are detected, each requiring RSI confirmation. Candlestick patterns (1-5) also require S/R confluence. Patterns are checked in priority order.

Pattern 1: Bullish Engulfing at Support

BUY CE — Bullish Engulfing

1
Previous candle is RED (bearish)
2
Current candle is GREEN and its body fully engulfs the previous candle's body
3
Current candle's low is within 15 pts of a swing low (support zone)
4
RSI > 45 (not deeply oversold — momentum supports move up)
5
Candle body ≥ 5 pts (filters out dojis)
SL: Signal candle low  |  Strength: STRONG (immediate entry)

Pattern 2: Bearish Engulfing at Resistance

BUY PE — Bearish Engulfing

1
Previous candle is GREEN (bullish)
2
Current candle is RED and its body fully engulfs the previous candle's body
3
Current candle's high is within 15 pts of a swing high (resistance zone)
4
RSI < 55 (not overbought — momentum supports move down)
5
Candle body ≥ 5 pts
SL: Signal candle high  |  Strength: STRONG

Pattern 3: Hammer (Pin Bar) at Support

BUY CE — Hammer

1
Lower wick ≥ 2× the body size (long rejection wick)
2
Upper wick ≤ 0.5× the body size (small upper shadow)
3
Candle low within support zone (15 pts of swing low)
4
RSI > 45  |  Body ≥ 2 pts minimum
SL: Pin bar low  |  Strength: MARGINAL (wait for close confirmation)

Pattern 4: Shooting Star (Bearish Pin Bar) at Resistance

BUY PE — Shooting Star

1
Upper wick ≥ 2× the body size (long rejection wick upward)
2
Lower wick ≤ 0.5× the body size
3
Candle high within resistance zone
4
RSI < 55  |  Body ≥ 2 pts minimum
SL: Pin bar high  |  Strength: MARGINAL

Pattern 5: Inside Bar Breakout

An inside bar is a candle whose entire range fits within the previous candle's range (the "mother candle"). It signals consolidation. Entry triggers when the next candle breaks out of the mother candle's range.

CE Breakout

1
Breakout candle closes ABOVE mother high
2
RSI > 45
SL: Mother candle low

PE Breakout

1
Breakout candle closes BELOW mother low
2
RSI < 55
SL: Mother candle high

Note: Inside Bar does NOT require S/R confluence (breakouts work anywhere). If no breakout occurs within 3 candles, the pending signal is cancelled.

Pattern 6: Break of Structure (BOS)

A BOS occurs when price closes beyond the most recent swing high or swing low, indicating a shift in market structure. This is the strongest signal type.

Bullish BOS

1
Candle closes ABOVE last swing high
2
Candle opened AT or BELOW that swing high
3
RSI > 45 + body ≥ 5 pts
SL: Recent swing low

Bearish BOS

1
Candle closes BELOW last swing low
2
Candle opened AT or ABOVE that swing low
3
RSI < 55 + body ≥ 5 pts
SL: Recent swing high

4. Entry Patterns — Chart Patterns (Multi-Candle)

These are multi-candle formations that require structural confirmation from swing points. They don't need S/R zone proximity — the pattern itself defines the key levels. All chart patterns use a configurable tolerance (PA_CHART_PATTERN_TOL, default 12 pts) to match swing point levels.

ParameterSetting KeyDefaultDescription
Pattern TolerancePA_CHART_PATTERN_TOL12 ptsMax difference between swing points for pattern match

Pattern 7: Double Top (Bearish Reversal)

Two swing highs form at approximately the same price level. The valley between them creates the neckline. Entry triggers when the current candle breaks below the neckline.

BUY PE — Double Top

1
Two recent swing highs within 12 pts tolerance of each other
2
At least 5 candles separation between the two tops (prevents noise)
3
Neckline = lowest low between the two swing highs
4
Current candle closes below neckline (opened at or above it)
5
RSI < 55  |  Body ≥ 5 pts
SL: Above the double top level (avg of two highs)  |  Strength: STRONG
How it works: Price tests a resistance level twice and fails both times. When it breaks below the valley (neckline) between those tests, it confirms sellers have taken control. This is a high-probability reversal pattern because it shows buyers exhausting at a clear level.

Pattern 8: Double Bottom (Bullish Reversal)

Two swing lows form at approximately the same price level. The peak between them creates the neckline. Entry triggers when the current candle breaks above the neckline.

BUY CE — Double Bottom

1
Two recent swing lows within 12 pts tolerance of each other
2
At least 5 candles separation between the two bottoms
3
Neckline = highest high between the two swing lows
4
Current candle closes above neckline (opened at or below it)
5
RSI > 45  |  Body ≥ 5 pts
SL: Below the double bottom level (avg of two lows)  |  Strength: STRONG
How it works: Price tests a support level twice and bounces both times. When it breaks above the peak (neckline) between those tests, it confirms buyers have regained control. The double test of support builds a strong floor.

Pattern 9: Ascending Triangle (Bullish Breakout)

A pattern with flat resistance (swing highs at similar levels) and rising support (each swing low is higher than the previous). Signals building buying pressure. Entry triggers on breakout above the flat resistance.

BUY CE — Ascending Triangle

1
Last 2 swing highs within 12 pts tolerance (flat resistance line)
2
Last 2 swing lows are rising (second low > first low)
3
Resistance level = average of the two flat swing highs
4
Current candle closes above resistance (opened at or below it)
5
RSI > 45  |  Body ≥ 5 pts
SL: Most recent rising swing low  |  Strength: STRONG
How it works: Buyers are consistently bidding at higher prices (higher lows), while sellers hold a fixed resistance. Eventually buyers overwhelm sellers and price breaks out upward. The rising lows show increasing demand — a strong bullish signal.

Pattern 10: Descending Triangle (Bearish Breakout)

A pattern with flat support (swing lows at similar levels) and falling resistance (each swing high is lower than the previous). Signals building selling pressure. Entry triggers on breakdown below the flat support.

BUY PE — Descending Triangle

1
Last 2 swing lows within 12 pts tolerance (flat support line)
2
Last 2 swing highs are falling (second high < first high)
3
Support level = average of the two flat swing lows
4
Current candle closes below support (opened at or above it)
5
RSI < 55  |  Body ≥ 5 pts
SL: Most recent falling swing high  |  Strength: STRONG
How it works: Sellers are consistently offering at lower prices (lower highs), while buyers hold a fixed support. Eventually sellers overwhelm buyers and price breaks down. The falling highs show decreasing demand — a strong bearish signal.

Clamping rule: SL distance = max(min(raw_distance, 25), 8). Too-tight SL on tiny candles is floored to 8 pts. Too-wide SL on volatile candles is capped at 25 pts.

6. Trailing Stop Loss (Swing-Based)

After entry, the SL is trailed using swing structure — moving the SL to the most recent swing low (for CE) or swing high (for PE). The trailing SL only tightens, never widens.

Trailing Rules

CE
CE (Long): Trail to latest swing low — must be ABOVE current SL AND below current price
PE
PE (Short): Trail to latest swing high — must be BELOW current SL AND above current price

Key: Only tightens. If new swing point would widen SL, it's ignored.

8. RSI as Confluence Filter

RSI is used as a lightweight confirmation, NOT the primary signal. It ensures momentum doesn't contradict the pattern.

CE Entry (Bullish)

RSI must be > 45. This means momentum isn't deeply bearish. It's permissive — RSI 46 is fine for a CE entry because the pattern itself is the signal.

Default: PA_RSI_CE_MIN = 45

PE Entry (Bearish)

RSI must be < 55. Momentum isn't deeply bullish. Same principle — the pattern leads, RSI confirms.

Default: PA_RSI_PE_MAX = 55

12. Practical Tips

Start with Backtest

Run /pa-backtest on the last 3-6 months first. Check win rate, drawdown, and which patterns perform best. Tune S/R zone width based on results.

Paper Trade First

Use /pa-paper for at least 2 weeks of live simulation. Watch how patterns fire in real-time. Note which setups you'd trust and which feel forced.

S/R Zone Tuning

If too few signals: increase PA_SR_ZONE_PTS (20-25). If too many false signals: decrease to 10. The zone width is the most impactful parameter.

Pattern Quality

Engulfing at S/R = highest conviction. BOS = strongest trend signal. Double Top/Bottom = best reversal. Triangles = best breakout. Pin bars = marginal (need strong wick). Inside bar = works best in volatility compression.
