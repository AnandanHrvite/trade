1 The idea in one minute

This strategy reads the shape of the NIFTY price chart and waits for one of four well-known patterns to complete. When it does, we buy a single option to ride the move that usually follows.

Traders have watched the same handful of chart shapes for a hundred years. When price traces one of them, it often signals which way the market is about to go. This strategy looks for exactly four of those shapes on a NIFTY chart made of 5-minute candles (each candle = 5 minutes of trading):

  • Two of them say "price is about to go up" → we buy a CE (call option), which makes money when NIFTY rises.
  • Two of them say "price is about to go down" → we buy a PE (put option), which makes money when NIFTY falls.

We only ever hold one option at a time — no pairs, no hedges. And there is no RSI, no ADX, no moving-average filter. The chart shape itself is the whole signal. The one extra check is that the candle which breaks the pattern has to be a decent-sized candle, not a tiny flicker.

The one habit that makes this strategy careful

We never buy the instant a pattern breaks. A lot of breakouts fail immediately and snap back — a trap. So after a pattern breaks, we wait for price to come back and touch the line it broke, then hold above (or below) it — a move called a retest. Only then do we buy. It means we sometimes miss a breakout that never comes back, but it keeps us out of most of the fakes.

3 Step 1 — Find the highs and lows

Before it can spot any pattern, the strategy first marks the recent little peaks and valleys on the chart. Everything else is built from these.

On every new candle, the strategy scans back over the last 30 candles (about two and a half hours) and marks:

  • Every swing high — a candle that stuck up higher than the candle on each side of it.
  • Every swing low — a candle that dipped lower than the candle on each side of it.

The two most recent swing highs act as the ceiling (resistance) and the two most recent swing lows act as the floor (support). When two of these peaks (or two valleys) sit at nearly the same price, that's the raw material for a pattern. "Nearly the same" means within 12 points of each other — the market rarely hits the exact same price twice, so we allow a little wiggle room.

How a peak or valley is decided

  • Swing high: a candle whose high is above both of its immediate neighbours' highs.
  • Swing low: a candle whose low is below both of its immediate neighbours' lows.
  • How far back we look: the last PA_SR_LOOKBACK = 30 candles, recomputed every new candle.

What we do with them

  • The last two swing highs = the "twin-top" / flat-ceiling reference.
  • The last two swing lows = the "twin-bottom" / flat-floor reference.
  • Two levels count as "equal" if within PA_CHART_PATTERN_TOL = 12 pts.
  • The same swings later drive the trailing stop (Section 5).

4 Step 2 — The four chart patterns

These are the only four setups the strategy trades. All four are switched on by default, and each can be turned off on its own. Two point up (buy a CE), two point down (buy a PE).

The only extra check: a real breakout candle

Beyond the shape itself there is no RSI, no ADX, no moving average. The single confluence check is that the candle which breaks the pattern has a body of at least 5 points (PA_MIN_BODY = 5) — a genuine push, not a tiny indecisive candle.

5 Step 3 — Wait for the retest, then buy

A pattern breaking is not enough on its own. We wait for price to come back, touch the line it broke, and hold on the breakout side — then we buy.

Think of the neckline (or the triangle's edge) like a door. When price bursts through, that's the breakout — but a lot of breakouts get sucked straight back inside. So instead of buying the moment the door opens, we wait to see price come back, tap the door from the other side, and stay out. That tap-and-hold is the retest, and it's our proof the level has genuinely flipped — the old ceiling is now acting as a floor, or the old floor is now a ceiling.

How the retest works

  • On a fresh breakout the strategy just remembers it (the level, the side, the pattern's stop). It does not buy yet.
  • Over the next few candles, price must pull back to within PA_RETEST_TOL_PTS = 10 pts of the broken level and then close back on the breakout side.
  • For a CE: a candle dips to the level (or 10 pts above it) but still closes above it → buy.
  • For a PE: a candle pokes up to the level (or 10 pts below it) but still closes below it → buy.
  • This is controlled by PA_RETEST_ENABLED = true. Turn it off and the strategy would buy the breakout candle itself.

When we give up on a breakout

  • It failed: price closes back through the level by more than 10 pts (the door slammed shut) → forget it.
  • It took too long: the retest doesn't show up within PA_RETEST_MAX_WAIT = 4 candles → forget it.
  • Out of hours: if the trading window ends while we're still waiting, we drop it.
Why bother waiting for the retest?

When this was tested, buying the raw breakout candle won only about 1 in 4 times — most breakouts reversed instantly. Waiting for price to come back and prove the level holds cuts out most of those fakes. The trade-off: sometimes a breakout rockets away and never comes back, and we simply miss it. That's accepted by design — we would rather skip a good one than take four bad ones.

6 Step 4 — Where we put the stop-loss

The safety exit sits just beyond the pattern — at the price that would prove the pattern wrong. Then it is squeezed into a sensible 8-to-25-point range.

Every trade gets a stop-loss the moment we buy — a price where, if NIFTY reaches it, we accept the setup failed and get out. We don't pick a random number; we place it at the structure that would break the pattern:

PatternSideStop sits…
Double Bottom (W)CEjust below the twin lows (if price falls back under them, the bottom failed)
Double Top (M)PEjust above the twin highs (if price climbs back over them, the top failed)
Ascending TriangleCEjust below the last rising low
Descending TrianglePEjust above the last falling high

The 3-point cushion and the 8–25 point squeeze

We place the stop 3 points beyond the pattern's edge so it isn't sitting exactly on the level (normal wiggle would trip it). Then we make sure the gap between our entry and the stop is never too tight nor too wide:

3-pt cushion

PA_SL_BUFFER_PTS = 3 — a little breathing room past the pattern extreme so an ordinary wobble doesn't stop us out.

Floor: 8 pts

PA_MIN_SL_PTS = 8 — if the structure sits closer than 8 points to our entry, we widen the stop to 8 so routine noise can't tag it.

Ceiling: 25 pts

PA_MAX_SL_PTS = 25 — on a tall pattern the structure could be far away, so we cap the risk at 25 points no matter what.

7 Step 5 — When we sell

There is no fixed profit target. Instead the stop-loss does the work: first it jumps to breakeven so the trade can't lose, then it trails up (or down) behind price to lock in more and more of a winner.

Stage 1 — Lift to breakeven

  • The first time the trade is up about ₹300 (PA_BREAKEVEN_TRIGGER = 300), the stop jumps to our entry price, give or take 1 point (PA_BREAKEVEN_BUFFER = 1).
  • From that moment a winner can no longer round-trip into a loss — the worst case is walking away flat.
  • ₹300 is a small amount — only about 4 points of option premium on one lot — so this protection arms early.

Stage 2 — Trail behind the swings

  • After breakeven, on each candle close the stop tightens to the latest swing low (for a CE) or latest swing high (for a PE).
  • It only ever moves the favourable way — it tightens, never loosens.
  • As long as price keeps making higher lows, the stop keeps ratcheting up under it, banking more of the move.
  • When a candle finally closes through the trailed stop, we're out with the profit locked in.

8 A worked example — a Double Bottom → CE

Real NIFTY-style numbers, start to finish, so you can see every rule fire in order.

10:05  NIFTY dips to a low of 24,180, bounces, then dips again to 24,190.
       Those two lows are 10 pts apart (inside the 12-pt "equal" window)
       and are 6 candles apart.  The highest point between them is 24,245.
       So: a Double Bottom is forming, with its NECKLINE at 24,245.

10:40  A 5-min candle opens at 24,246 and CLOSES at 24,254 — above the
       neckline (24,245).  Its body is 8 pts (over the 5-pt minimum).
       → Valid Double Bottom breakout.  We REMEMBER it. We do NOT buy yet.

10:50  Price drifts back down and a candle dips to 24,248 (within 10 pts
       of the neckline) but still CLOSES at 24,251 — back above 24,245.
       → RETEST confirmed.  BUY an at-the-money CE at a premium of ₹115.

       Stop-loss: just below the twin lows (24,180) − 3-pt cushion = 24,177.
       That's 74 pts below entry — wider than the 25-pt cap — so the stop
       is pulled up to 24,251 − 25 = 24,226.

11:30  NIFTY has climbed and the position is about ₹300 in profit
       → stop jumps to entry + 1 = 24,252 (breakeven). Can't lose now.

12:15  Price keeps making higher lows; each new swing low drags the stop
       up behind it — now around 24,285. We just hold and let it run.

13:20  A candle finally CLOSES below the trailed stop → SELL.
       The CE is now worth ₹175.  Profit = ₹60 per unit; on one lot
       (75 units) that's about ₹4,500 before charges.  Win banked.
What to notice
  • The breakout at 10:40 did not get us in — the retest at 10:50 did. That patience is the whole point.
  • The natural stop was 74 pts away, but the 25-pt cap kept our risk small.
  • Once ₹300 up, the trade was risk-free; from there the swing trail decided the size of the win. No target ever capped it.

9 The "market too jumpy" filter (VIX)

An optional filter that tells the strategy to sit out when the market's fear gauge is running hot. It's off by default.

VIX is the market's fear gauge. When it's high, options get expensive and price swings get violent — a riskier time to trade. If you switch this filter on, the strategy simply refuses to enter a new trade while VIX is above a ceiling you set.

How it's set up

  • Switch it on with PA_VIX_ENABLED = true (default is off).
  • The ceiling is PA_VIX_MAX_ENTRY = 20 (if you leave it blank it falls back to the shared VIX_MAX_ENTRY).
  • If VIX data can't be fetched, the safe default is to block the trade (VIX_FAIL_MODE = closed). Set it to open to allow trading when VIX is unknown.
  • Because every pattern here counts as "strong", there is no separate "only take strong setups" tier for PA — it's simply trade / don't trade.

Where the number comes from

  • Live / Paper: reads India VIX from Fyers, refreshed about once a minute.
  • Backtest: uses the historical VIX for each day.
  • The check happens after a pattern has produced a signal, and only in live/paper (it's skipped in replay simulations).

10 Two more checks before we buy

Even after a pattern, retest, and VIX pass, a live/paper trade has two last common-sense checks — plus a safety net that survives a crash.

The buy/sell gap must be tight (spread guard)

Every option has a bid-ask spread — the gap between what you can sell at and what you must pay. A wide gap quietly eats your profit the moment you enter and exit. So if that gap is wider than MAX_BID_ASK_SPREAD_PTS = 2 points, we skip the trade. (Live and paper only.)

The crowd-positioning check (OI)

Open interest (OI) shows where the big money is building positions. This optional filter (PA_OI_ENABLED = false, and it also needs the master OI switch on) can veto an entry that goes against a strong build-up. It's off by default. (Live and paper only.)

If the server restarts mid-trade (crash recovery)

  • An open position is saved to disk (~/trading-data/.active_pa_position.json) the moment it opens, so a restart, crash, or reload doesn't lose track of it — on boot it's read straight back.
  • The save is dated. If the saved position is from a previous day, it's thrown away on boot (an intraday position can't survive overnight), so there's no phantom recovery.
  • A breakout that's still waiting for its retest is not saved — after a restart it's simply re-spotted from fresh candles.

12 Paper, Live & Backtest

The strategy runs three ways. All three share the exact same brain — the four patterns, the retest, the structural stop, and the breakeven-then-trail exit. Paper is the reference the other two are checked against.

Paper — /pa-paper (practice)

Real live prices, pretend money. No real orders are ever placed. This is the "official" version of the strategy — when live or backtest behave differently, paper is treated as correct. Trades are saved to disk and survive a restart; real option prices are used for profit tracking. Practice capital comes from FYERS_INV_AMOUNT (default ₹1,00,000, shared across Fyers strategies).

Live — /pa-live (real money)

Real orders through Fyers — but locked behind two safety switches so it can never fire by accident: PA_ENABLED must be on and the global dry-run switch LIVE_HARNESS_DRY_RUN must be off. While dry-run is on (the default), the system shows the order it would place but sends nothing. The /pa-live-harness route runs live by wrapping the paper engine, so live decisions match paper by construction.

Backtest — /pa-backtest (test on the past)

Runs the strategy over past dates to see how it would have done. History has no real option prices, so the backtest estimates the option: it assumes the option moves about 0.55 point for every NIFTY point (delta) and loses about 10 points of value per day to time-decay (theta), then subtracts brokerage and an optional slippage. So backtest profit is a good approximation, not the exact fills you'd get live — always sanity-check against paper. There's also a per-pattern view (/pa-pattern-backtest) that shows how each of the four patterns did on its own.

14 Good to know

A few reminders to keep in mind while running or tuning the strategy.

A sensible way to start
  • Try it on history first: /pa-backtest (and /pa-pattern-backtest to see each pattern on its own).
  • Then paper-trade for 2+ weeks: /pa-paper (this is the reference).
  • Only flip the live dry-run off after checking that live and paper make the same calls on a recorded day via /replay.
If you want to tune it
  • Fewer, cleaner signals: lower PA_CHART_PATTERN_TOL (twin highs/lows must be more equal) or raise PA_MIN_BODY.
  • More fills: raise PA_RETEST_TOL_PTS or PA_RETEST_MAX_WAIT. Fewer false fills: tighten them.
  • Wider / tighter risk: adjust PA_MAX_SL_PTS and PA_MIN_SL_PTS.
  • Only tune on clean post-rebuild sessions — older trades came from different code.

The whole strategy in six lines
  • It reads pure chart shape — no RSI, no ADX. The only extra check is a real (≥ 5-pt) breakout candle.
  • It trades four patterns only: Double Bottom / Double Top / Ascending Triangle / Descending Triangle.
  • It never buys the breakout — it waits for the retest to prove the level holds.
  • The stop sits at the pattern structure, squeezed to 8–25 points.
  • It has no profit target: breakeven first, then a swing trail lets winners run.
  • Old trade logs mentioning Engulfing / Pin Bar / BOS / Inside Bar are history — those aren't traded anymore.
