1 Strategy Overview

PRICE_ACTION_5M reads raw price structure on 5-minute NIFTY candles. It is a pure chart-pattern breakout strategy — no RSI, no ADX, no lagging indicators. The pattern breakout candle (confirmed by a retest) IS the signal.

Why 5-Minute Candles?

  • 3x more opportunities vs 15-min timeframe
  • Cleaner chart patterns — W / M tops and triangles resolve faster
  • Structural SL sits at the pattern's invalidation level, capped to 25 pts
  • Zero indicator lag — reads what price IS doing
  • Swing-based S/R from dynamic swing highs/lows (30-candle lookback)
  • Retest confirmation filters out breakout-then-instant-reversal false signals

2 Support & Resistance Detection

Swing highs and swing lows within the last 30 candles are the structural anchors that all four chart patterns are built from. The same swing points also drive the structure trail.

Swing Point Rules

  • Swing High: Candle whose high is greater than both its immediate neighbors' highs
  • Swing Low: Candle whose low is less than both its immediate neighbors' lows
  • Lookback: PA_SR_LOOKBACK = 30 candles
  • Computed by findSwingPoints() in price_action.js; recalculated every new candle

How Swing Points Are Used

  • The last two swing highs form the flat-resistance / twin-top reference
  • The last two swing lows form the flat-support / twin-bottom reference
  • "Equal" levels are matched within PA_CHART_PATTERN_TOL = 12 pts
  • The same swings feed the structure trail (latest swing low for CE, swing high for PE)
  • There is no fixed S/R "zone" band — the old PA_SR_ZONE_PTS concept was removed in the rebuild

4 Entry Confirmation — Retest

A confirmed pattern breakout does not enter on the breakout candle. It is parked as a pending breakout and only fires when price returns to the broken level and closes back on the breakout side — a retest. This filters out breakout-then-instant-reversal false signals.

How the Retest Works

  • On a fresh breakout, the engine stores the level, side, pattern, and structural SL as a pending breakout (it does not buy).
  • Each subsequent candle, price must pull back to within PA_RETEST_TOL_PTS = 10 pts of the broken level and close back on the breakout side.
  • CE (bullish): bar low ≤ level + 10 and close > level → enter.
  • PE (bearish): bar high ≥ level − 10 and close < level → enter.
  • Controlled by PA_RETEST_ENABLED = true (default). If turned off, the breakout candle enters immediately.

When a Pending Breakout Is Dropped

  • Failed breakout: price closes back through the level by more than 10 pts (CE closes below level−10, PE closes above level+10) → discarded.
  • Timed out: the retest is not seen within PA_RETEST_MAX_WAIT = 4 candles → discarded.
  • Outside window: if the trading window closes while a breakout is pending, the pending state is dropped.
  • All three knobs are internal (no Settings rows); override via .env / Bulk-Edit if needed.
Why retest instead of breakout-candle entry?

A replay diagnostic over 8 sessions showed raw breakout-candle entries won only ~23% of the time (largely breakout-then-instant-reversal). The retest waits for price to prove the broken level now holds as support/resistance before committing, at the cost of occasionally missing breakouts that never come back.

5 Stop Loss Rules — Structural, Capped

The initial SL is placed at the pattern's invalidation level (the structure that, if broken, says the breakout failed), then clamped to a range of 8–25 points to balance noise and risk. All four patterns are STRONG.

Pattern Side Structural SL Anchor
Double Bottom (W) CE Below the double-bottom level (twin lows)
Double Top (M) PE Above the double-top level (twin highs)
Ascending Triangle CE Below the rising swing low
Descending Triangle PE Above the falling swing high

SL Placement & Clamp

For CE: raw = structLevel − PA_SL_BUFFER_PTS, then gap = clamp(entryClose − raw, [8, 25]), SL = entryClose − gap. PE is the mirror image. (See _clampedSL() in price_action.js.)

Buffer beyond structure: 3 pts

PA_SL_BUFFER_PTS = 3 — small cushion past the pattern extreme so the stop isn't sitting exactly on the level.

Min SL floor: 8 pts

PA_MIN_SL_PTS = 8 — if the structure is closer than 8 pts, the SL widens to 8 to avoid getting stopped on normal noise.

Max SL cap: 25 pts

PA_MAX_SL_PTS = 25 — on tall patterns the structural distance is capped at 25 pts to bound per-trade loss. (The old 8–12 pt clamp and the PA_MAX_STRUCT_SL_PTS skip were removed.)

6 Breakeven, then Swing-Based Trail

Stop management has two stages: a one-time breakeven lift once the trade is in profit, then a swing-structure trail that tightens to each new swing point. Both only ever move the SL the favourable way — they never widen it.

Stage 1 — Breakeven Lift

  • Triggered the first time peak PnL ≥ PA_BREAKEVEN_TRIGGER = 300 ₹ (set to 0 to disable).
  • SL is lifted to entry spot ± PA_BREAKEVEN_BUFFER = 1 pt (above entry for CE, below for PE) — a winner can no longer round-trip into a loss.
  • Applied per tick by the route engine (onTick in paPaper.js), and only if the breakeven level is tighter than the current SL.
  • Arms once per trade; the swing trail keeps tightening from there.

7 Exit Logic — Priority Order

Exits are checked in strict priority. The first condition that triggers wins.

1

SL Hit (every tick)

Price touches the current SL — the initial structural stop, the breakeven level, or the latest swing-trailed level. Checked on every incoming tick for immediate protection. CE exits when price ≤ SL; PE exits when price ≥ SL.

2

Breakeven Lift (every tick)

Once peak PnL ≥ PA_BREAKEVEN_TRIGGER (₹300), the SL is lifted once to entry ± PA_BREAKEVEN_BUFFER (1 pt). This isn't an exit by itself — it tightens the SL so the next adverse tick can't take the trade red. Only moves the SL the favourable way.

3

Swing Trail SL Update (candle close)

At each candle close (with ≥ 15 completed candles), tighten the SL to the latest swing low (CE) / swing high (PE). Only tightens — never widens. A subsequent tick hitting this tightened SL exits via priority 1.

4

EOD Square-off (~3:20 PM IST)

Open positions are squared off 10 minutes before the global stop time (TRADE_STOP_TIME, default 15:30 → square-off ≈ 15:20). No overnight risk for this intraday options strategy.

8c Position Persistence — Crash Recovery NEW

Active positions are persisted to disk so they survive PM2 restarts, OOM kills, and unexpected crashes.

How It Works

  • State saved to ~/trading-data/.active_pa_position.json
  • Atomic writes via tmp + rename — no corrupted files on crash
  • Saves: side, symbol, qty, entry price, SL, best price, order ID, option details
  • Cleared automatically when position closes
  • An open position is what survives a restart. A breakout still awaiting its retest (the in-memory _pendingBreakout) is not persisted — it is dropped on restart and re-detected from fresh candles.

Stale Position Guard

  • Each save records the current IST date
  • On boot, if the saved date is not today, the file is discarded — stale positions from yesterday are invalid for intraday
  • Prevents phantom recovery of expired positions
  • Crash recovery handles Swing / Scalp / PA (each with its own persist file). ORB does not yet have a crash-recovery snapshot.

14 Practical Tips

Recommendations for deploying and tuning the Price Action strategy.

Getting Started
  • Start with backtest: /pa-backtest
  • Per-pattern attribution: /pa-pattern-backtest (one panel per pattern)
  • Paper trade for 2+ weeks: /pa-paper (canonical)
  • Validate that Paper and Live decisions match on a recorded session via /replay before flipping the live harness OFF
Tuning Levers (all internal / Bulk-Edit)
  • Fewer / cleaner signals: tighten PA_CHART_PATTERN_TOL (lower) so twin tops/bottoms must be more "equal", or raise PA_MIN_BODY.
  • More retest fills: raise PA_RETEST_TOL_PTS or PA_RETEST_MAX_WAIT; fewer false fills: tighten them.
  • Wider / tighter stops: adjust PA_MAX_SL_PTS (cap) and PA_MIN_SL_PTS (floor).
  • Collect clean post-rebuild sessions before tuning thresholds — pre-rebuild trades came from different code.

The Four Patterns

Double Bottom (W)
→ CE
Double Top (M)
→ PE
Ascending Triangle
→ CE
Descending Triangle
→ PE

All four are STRONG and ON by default. Each is individually toggleable: PA_PATTERN_DOUBLE_BOTTOM, PA_PATTERN_DOUBLE_TOP, PA_PATTERN_ASC_TRIANGLE, PA_PATTERN_DESC_TRIANGLE.

Important Reminders
  • PA is pure price structure — there is no RSI gate and no ADX filter. The only confluence beyond the pattern is the breakout-candle body (PA_MIN_BODY = 5 pts).
  • A breakout never enters on the breakout candle — it must retest the broken level first (PA_RETEST_*). Setups that don't come back are missed by design.
  • The initial SL sits at the pattern structure, clamped to [8, 25] pts. Exit management is breakeven (≥ ₹PA_BREAKEVEN_TRIGGER) then swing-structure trail — no candle-trail, no tiered profit-lock, no time-stop.
  • The PA VIX filter is off by default and, when on, is effectively a single max-entry block (every PA signal is STRONG, so the strong-only tier never bites).
  • VIX failure mode is fail-closed by default — if VIX data is unavailable, entries are blocked. Set VIX_FAIL_MODE=open to reverse.
  • Old trade logs with Engulfing / BOS / Pin Bar / Inside Bar names are history — those patterns are no longer detected or traded.
