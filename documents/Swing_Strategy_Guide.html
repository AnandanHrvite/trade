03 — Trading Modes

The Swing strategy runs in three distinct modes. All three share the same signal logic (9 Gate Pipeline, 3-Tier Trailing SL, VIX Filter) but differ in data source, order execution, and persistence.

Mode 2

Identical signal logic and exit rules. Simulated order fills at market price. Tracks PnL, win rate, and session history. Same code path as live.

04 — Entry Logic — 9 Gate Pipeline

Every potential entry must pass all 9 gates sequentially. A failure at any gate blocks the signal. The order is optimized to reject cheaply — the most common rejections happen at gates 1-3.

1
EMA9 Touch
Price must pull back to and touch the 9-period EMA within the candle
CE: High ≥ EMA9 AND Low ≤ EMA9 + 20  |  PE: Low ≤ EMA9 AND High ≥ EMA9 - 20
2
SAR Position
Parabolic SAR must confirm trend direction (3 valid logic paths)
CE: trend=1 (dots below)  |  PE: trend=-1 (dots above)  |  SAR flip  |  Bull Override
3
EMA9 Slope
EMA must be actively moving in trade direction — flat EMAs produce noise signals
CE: slope ≥ +6 pts vs prev candle  |  PE: slope ≤ -6 pts vs prev candle
4
Trend Filter
Align with medium-term trend — eliminates counter-trend trades (biggest losers)
CE: Close > EMA30 AND EMA9 > EMA21  |  PE: Close < EMA30 AND EMA9 < EMA21
5
Candle Body
Candle must close in the trade direction with minimum body size (no dojis)
CE: Close > Open, body ≥ 10pts  |  PE: Close < Open, body ≥ 10pts
6
SAR Distance
Stop loss (SAR value) must be in the optimal range — not too tight, not too wide
Min: 45 pts  |  Max: 200 pts   (entry price to SAR SL)
7
ADX Strength
Market must be trending, not ranging — EMA touches in choppy markets are noise
ADX(14) ≥ 25   (trending market confirmed)
8
RSI Momentum
Momentum must confirm the trade direction — dead zone 48-52 blocked
CE: RSI > 52  |  PE: RSI < 48
9
VIX Filter
India VIX must be within acceptable range — high VIX = erratic moves, SLs blown
VIX ≤ 20 to enter  |  VIX 16-20: STRONG signals only  |  VIX > 20: all entries blocked
Pipeline efficiency: ~70% of ticks are rejected at Gate 1 (no EMA touch), ~20% at Gates 2-3 (wrong SAR/slope). Only ~10% reach the deeper gates, keeping CPU usage minimal.

05 — SAR Position — Three Entry Logics

Gate 2 (SAR Position) has three valid paths. The SAR does not need to flip for an entry — it just needs to be on the correct side.

Logic 1 — SAR Already Positioned
Standard Entry
SAR has been on the correct side for multiple candles. This is the most common and reliable entry type.
CE
SAR dots below candles (trend=1)
PE
SAR dots above candles (trend=-1)
Logic 2 — SAR Just Flipped
Trend Reversal Entry
SAR changed direction on THIS candle. Previous candle had opposite trend. Catches trend reversals at the earliest point.
CE: prevSAR.trend=-1 AND currSAR.trend=1
PE: prevSAR.trend=1 AND currSAR.trend=-1
Logic 3 — Bull Override (PE only)
Momentum Override
SAR still bullish but strong downward momentum detected. SAR is lagging behind a fast move. Uses EMA9 as SL instead of SAR.
EMA9 slope ≤ -6 pts
Close < EMA9
RSI < 42
(Close - SAR) > 50 pts
SL = EMA9 value
Logic 3 also has a CE mirror: SAR still bearish but strong bullish momentum. EMA9 slope ≥ +6, Close > EMA9, RSI > 53, (SAR - Close) > 30pts. SL = EMA9.

10 — Trailing Stop Loss — 3-Tier Dynamic Gap System

The trailing SL gap adjusts dynamically based on how far the trade has moved in favour. This captures profits without being too tight during the initial move or too loose during a runner.

1
Move < 30 pts
60 pt gap
Wide gap — room to breathe
Early move, don't choke it
2
Move 30-70 pts
25 pt gap
Tight gap — lock profits
Good move, protect it
3
Move ≥ 70 pts
30 pt gap
Slight widen — let it run
Healthy retracement room

Trail Activation

Formula: trailActivatePts = min(max(SARgap × 0.25, 12), 40)

The trail only starts after the trade moves a minimum distance. This distance is proportional to the initial SAR gap (SL distance), ensuring the trail doesn't activate too early on wide-SL trades.

SAR Gap (SL distance)CalculationTrail Activates At
45 ptsmax(45 × 0.25, 12) = max(11.25, 12)+12 pts
80 ptsmax(80 × 0.25, 12) = max(20, 12)+20 pts
120 ptsmax(120 × 0.25, 12) = max(30, 12)+30 pts
200 ptsmin(max(200 × 0.25, 12), 40) = min(50, 40)+40 pts (capped)
Key rule: The trailing SL can only tighten (move towards price). It never loosens. Once moved to breakeven, it stays there or gets tighter.

12 — Breakeven Stop Mechanics

When the trade moves +25 pts in favour, the stop loss is immediately moved to the entry price. This eliminates risk on the position while letting the trail system handle profit protection.

13a — Hybrid Initial SL Cap v4.4.0

Initial SL on Swing was historically SAR-based only — on young trends the SAR can sit 100–130 pts away, so a single losing trade could wipe out multiple winners. v4.4.0 adds a per-trade hybrid cap that picks the tightest structurally-justified SL and floors it so it can't go suicide-tight on doji bars.

Computation

candidates = [
   sarStopLoss,                             // raw SAR (preserved on trade record)
   prev-candle low/high (if SWING_USE_PREV_CANDLE_SL ),
   entry ± SWING_MAX_INITIAL_SL_PTS         // hard cap
]

stopLoss   = tightest of candidates  // min for CE, max for PE
stopLoss   = floor at entry ± SWING_MIN_INITIAL_SL_PTS

Configuration

VariableDefaultNotes
SWING_USE_PREV_CANDLE_SLtrueInclude prev-candle structural low/high in the min-of stack
SWING_MAX_INITIAL_SL_PTS50Hard cap on initial SL distance — binds when SAR alone would be wider
SWING_MIN_INITIAL_SL_PTS15Floor for initial SL distance — protects against suicide-tight SLs on doji bars

Side-Effects

  • Trail activation rescaled. TRAIL_ACTIVATE_PTS previously could be dwarfed by a wide SAR-only SL. With the cap binding, the trail-activation knob actually controls when the 3-tier trailer engages.
  • New trade-record field sarStopLoss preserves the raw SAR distance at entry, so paper-vs-live and paper-vs-historical analyses can compare against the pre-cap value.
  • Wired into Swing Paper + Swing Live on both candle-close and intra-candle entry paths via the shared _applyInitialSLCap() helper. Backtest is intentionally untouched during the paper-trade data-collection window so historical baseline stays comparable.
  • Settings UI. All 3 knobs are exposed in the Swing section; SAVE ALL writes them straight to .env.

14b — Trade Guards — Bid-Ask Spread + Time-Stop v4.3.0

Two shared guards live in src/utils/tradeGuards.js and are consulted by Swing, Scalp, and PA paper + live engines.

Bid-Ask Spread Guard (entry)

Before placing an entry, the option's bid/ask spread is compared to MAX_BID_ASK_SPREAD_PTS (default 2). If wider, the entry is rejected with reason WIDE_SPREAD. The check fails open if quotes are unavailable, so live entries don't freeze on a transient broker outage.

Time-Stop (exit)

Any open trade that has stayed flat (|PnL| < TIME_STOP_FLAT_PTS) for TIME_STOP_CANDLES consecutive candles is auto-exited with reason TIME_STOP. This prevents pure theta drain on directionless trades. Defaults: 4 candles / 20 pts. PA tightens to 3 / 10 via PA_TIME_STOP_CANDLES/PA_TIME_STOP_FLAT_PTS.

14c — Per-Filter Near-Miss Audit v4.3.0

Every candle-close evaluation produces a filterAudit structure listing which gates passed and which failed. src/utils/nearMissLog.js logs every candle that missed entry by exactly one filter (configurable threshold), tagging the failing filter name and detail (e.g. ADX 18.4 < 20). View live in the /logs SSE feed.

Use this to quantify the opportunity cost of each filter: if "ADX gate" misses 30 trades a week and most would have been winners, you have data to support relaxing it. The Paper Trade Data Collection window is exactly when this audit is most valuable.
