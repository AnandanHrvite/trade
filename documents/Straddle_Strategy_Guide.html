🎯 1. Strategy Overview

Long Straddle is a non-directional intraday volatility strategy. The thesis: when implied volatility is unusually low (BB bands have squeezed below their recent average), or when VIX is at a cheap-premium floor, a directional move — in either direction — is more likely to be cheap to play. Buying both the ATM CE and ATM PE simultaneously means the position profits when the underlying moves significantly in either direction, and loses if the underlying chops sideways and theta bleeds both legs.

This is the only strategy in the suite that places paired orders. Both legs (CE + PE) are placed sequentially at strategy fire. P&L is tracked on the combined premium against the net debit paid (entry sum of both legs). Exits also fire on the combined premium — never on a single leg.

Why a Straddle and Not a Strangle?

A straddle (same strike) has higher net debit but a tighter break-even than a strangle (different strikes). On NIFTY index, intraday moves are typically 50–100 points; with ATM premiums in the ₹80–150 range each, a straddle's break-even is realistic intraday, while a strangle's wider OTM strikes require larger moves to clear net debit. The strategy's exit is set at a fraction of net debit (default +20%) — capturing the rare gamma pop early rather than holding for a home run.

🔗 2. BB-Squeeze Trigger

The primary entry signal is a Bollinger Band squeeze: the current BB width is significantly tighter than its recent average, which mathematically describes a period of compressed realised volatility. Mean reversion of volatility — on average it expands after compression — is the directional thesis the straddle exploits.

VariableDefaultDescription
STRADDLE_BB_PERIOD20Bollinger Band SMA period (candles)
STRADDLE_BB_STDDEV2Bollinger Band std deviation multiplier
STRADDLE_BB_AVG_LOOKBACK20Number of bars to average BB width over (squeeze comparison baseline)
STRADDLE_SQUEEZE_RATIO0.85Trigger fires when current BB width ≤ this fraction of the lookback average

Squeeze Math

bbWidth(t)    = bbUpper(t) - bbLower(t)
avgWidth      = SMA(bbWidth, STRADDLE_BB_AVG_LOOKBACK)

squeeze_fires = (bbWidth(t) / avgWidth) ≤ STRADDLE_SQUEEZE_RATIO

With defaults: squeeze fires when current BB width is ≤ 85% of the 20-bar average.

What the squeeze looks like in an entry-reason string

BB squeeze | BBwidth=0.0023 avg=0.0028 | VIX=15.4 [STRONG]

The trigger string surfaces the actual current width, the lookback average, and VIX at fire time. This format is the Straddle fingerprint — cross-strategy disambiguation uses this to route the trade to the right analysis bucket.

Skip States

  • "BB indicator not ready" — warming up, fewer than STRADDLE_BB_PERIOD candles available.
  • "Warming up (X/25 candles for BB squeeze detection)" — have BB but not enough lookback history to compute the average yet.
  • "No trigger — BB width X vs avg Y (squeeze<0.85 of avg), VIX Z >= 14 cheap-threshold, force=false" — primary skip path. Surfaces all three gating conditions at once so the operator can see exactly why nothing fired.

⚡ 3. Two Entry Paths — Squeeze vs Cheap-VIX

An entry can fire via two independent paths. Either is sufficient on its own — the strategy ORs the two paths, not ANDs.

PathConditionWhy
1. BB Squeeze bbWidth ≤ STRADDLE_SQUEEZE_RATIO × avgWidth Realised volatility has compressed. Straddle plays the mean-reversion (expansion).
2. Cheap VIX VIX < STRADDLE_VIX_CHEAP (default 14) Implied volatility is at a cheap floor — option premiums are unusually low. Even without a BB squeeze, the asymmetric R:R of a long straddle becomes attractive.

The two-path design is deliberate. BB-squeeze captures realised compression but can lag — by the time the squeeze fires, the move may already be underway. The cheap-VIX path catches implied compression — you're buying volatility while it's cheap by historical standards, before any structural compression has shown up in the BB envelope.

VIX Gating (independent of cheap-VIX path)

Separately, ALL straddle entries are subject to a VIX upper bound. If VIX is too high, premiums are pumped and the long-straddle R:R deteriorates.

VariableDefaultEffect
STRADDLE_VIX_ENABLEDtrueMaster toggle for the VIX upper bound. Defaults on — the strategy is most exposed to VIX of all five.
STRADDLE_VIX_MAX_ENTRY22Above this VIX, all straddle entries are blocked — premiums pumped, poor R:R.
STRADDLE_VIX_STRONG_ONLY18Between 18 and 22, only STRONG-classified squeeze triggers fire. MARGINAL squeezes blocked.
STRADDLE_VIX_CHEAP14Below this VIX, the cheap-VIX path fires (additionally to BB-squeeze).
Net VIX behaviour: the sweet spot for straddle is VIX ≈ 14–18. Above 18 you need a STRONG squeeze; above 22 you can't trade at all; below 14 the cheap-premium path opens up additional entries even without a squeeze.

▶ 4. Entry Mechanics — Paired CE + PE

When all gates pass and a trigger fires, the strategy enters both legs simultaneously at the nearest ATM strike (per STRIKE_OFFSET_CE / STRIKE_OFFSET_PE — defaults 0 = pure ATM).

CE Leg (Call)

Buy 1 lot ATM CE.

  • Strike: ATM + STRIKE_OFFSET_CE
  • Side: BUY
  • Qty: NIFTY_LOT_SIZE × LOT_MULTIPLIER
  • Order type: market (paper) / market (live)
PE Leg (Put)

Buy 1 lot ATM PE.

  • Strike: ATM + STRIKE_OFFSET_PE
  • Side: BUY
  • Qty: NIFTY_LOT_SIZE × LOT_MULTIPLIER
  • Order type: market (paper) / market (live)

Sequential Order Placement (Live)

In Live mode, the two legs are placed sequentially via the Fyers broker, not as a bracket. CE is placed first; on confirmation, PE is placed. The sequence is intentional — market-orders take ~50–200 ms each via the Fyers API, so doing them serially gives a clean per-leg confirmation rather than racing them.

Partial-fill protection: if the PE order fails (broker error, rejection, network) after the CE order has filled, the strategy does not auto-unwind the CE leg. Instead it logs an error and sends a Telegram alert that you have a one-sided position open. You decide whether to unwind manually or hold — the strategy will continue to track the CE as a long call until you handle it. This is intentionally conservative; an auto-unwind path could create double-slippage on a fast-moving market.

Entry-Time Constraints

🧮 5. Net-Debit P&L Math

The straddle's P&L tracking is different from every other strategy in this suite. It is not tracked per-leg; it is tracked on the combined premium of both legs against the original net debit paid.

netDebit       = entry_CE_premium + entry_PE_premium
combined(t)    = current_CE_premium + current_PE_premium

pnl(t)         = (combined(t) − netDebit) × lot_qty
pnl_pct(t)     = (combined(t) / netDebit) − 1

Worked Example

NIFTY at 24,000. ATM = 24000. Lot size = 65, multiplier = 2.

CE entry: 100 ₹ per option
PE entry: 95 ₹ per option
netDebit = 100 + 95 = 195 ₹
qty      = 65 × 2 = 130

NIFTY moves to 24,100 (CE = 145, PE = 60):
combined  = 145 + 60 = 205 ₹
pnl       = (205 − 195) × 130 = +₹1,300
pnl_pct   = (205 / 195) - 1 = +5.1%

The position is profitable even though PE has lost 35 ₹/leg, because CE has gained 45 ₹/leg.
This is the volatility-expansion thesis playing out: a unidirectional move that's large enough.

If NIFTY had stayed flat at 24,000 for two hours instead:
CE = 93, PE = 88, combined = 181 (theta decay on both)
pnl = (181 − 195) × 130 = −₹1,820
pnl_pct = -7.2%

This is the failure mode: chop kills both legs simultaneously via theta.
Why combined-premium tracking matters: tracking each leg independently can produce misleading signals (e.g. "the PE is at −30%, exit it"). On a long straddle, the PE losing premium while CE gains more is the desired outcome. Both legs must be evaluated together.

📍 6. Exit Logic — Combined Premium

Exits are evaluated every tick on the combined premium, with the first matching condition firing the exit.

  1. Target hit — combined ≥ netDebit × (1 + STRADDLE_TARGET_PCT)
    Default 0.20 → exit at +20% net. Locks the rare gamma pop early.
  2. SL hit — combined ≤ netDebit × (1 − STRADDLE_STOP_PCT)
    Default 0.30 → exit at −30% net. Wider SL absorbs intraday noise.
  3. Max hold time — holdDays ≥ STRADDLE_MAX_HOLD_DAYS
    Default 0.3 → intraday-only (paper trade auto-stops at EOD anyway; this prevents surprise force-close).
  4. Forced EOD exit — now ≥ STRADDLE_FORCED_EXIT (default 15:15)

Why Target < SL (0.20 < 0.30)?

The default target (+20%) is tighter than the default SL (−30%). On the face of it, this looks like a negative-expectancy setup. But the long-straddle distribution is not symmetric: the probability of hitting +20% is higher than the probability of hitting −30% on the days the strategy actually fires (BB-squeeze + low VIX). Most squeeze breakouts produce a 20–30% expansion within the first hour; the trade exits with a small win. The −30% SL covers the failure mode where chop continues and theta bleeds both legs — needing a wider SL is correct precisely because the chop is slow.

That said, the defaults are conservative for the data-collection window. Once the window closes and you have a population of straddle outcomes to analyse, retune both numbers.

No per-leg SL. Unlike PA / Scalp / Swing, there is no spot-based stop on either leg individually. The CE could be down 50% while the PE is up 80% — the strategy holds. Combined-premium is the only exit signal.

⚡ 7. Force-Entry Override (Event Days)

For RBI policy days, Union Budget, major results, or other scheduled events where you know volatility will expand but the BB squeeze hasn't formed in the data yet, the strategy supports a one-shot manual override.

VariableDefaultDescription
STRADDLE_FORCE_ENTRY_NEXT false When set to true, the next candle close inside the entry window triggers an entry regardless of BB/VIX gates. The flag is auto-reset to false after firing — so it's strictly one-shot.

Usage Pattern

  1. Open Settings before market open on the event day.
  2. Toggle STRADDLE_FORCE_ENTRY_NEXT → true. Save.
  3. Start the Straddle Paper or Live session.
  4. On the first candle close inside the entry window (09:30 ≤ t < 11:00), the strategy enters regardless of BB/VIX state. The override flag flips back to false immediately after firing.
  5. The trade proceeds through the normal exit stack (target / SL / time-stop / EOD).
The override does NOT bypass the entry-time window or the daily pair cap. If you flip it on outside of 09:30–11:00, nothing fires until the window opens. If you've already taken your STRADDLE_MAX_DAILY_PAIRS for the day, the override still respects the cap.

⚙ 9. Operating Modes

Paper Mode — /straddle-paper

Real-time Fyers tick feed via socketManager; simulated paired fills. Canonical decision/fill/exit logic — the source of truth. Sessions persist to ~/trading-data/straddle_paper_trades.json; per-day JSONL audit at ~/trading-data/trades/straddle_paper_trades_YYYY-MM-DD.jsonl (seeded with a settings snapshot at session start). Each entry record includes both legs (pos.ce + pos.pe), the net debit, and combined-premium tracking over the life of the trade.

Live Mode — /straddle-live

Real Fyers orders, both legs placed sequentially. Double-gated: both STRADDLE_LIVE_ENABLED=true AND LIVE_HARNESS_DRY_RUN=false are required. With DRY-RUN on (default), the broker calls are logged but no real orders are placed. Switch DRY-RUN off only after verifying Live and Paper decisions match on a recorded session via /replay.

Backtest Mode — /straddle-backtest

Date-range backtest replaying historical NIFTY candles + option chains. The backtest records option LTP polls during the run so Replay can reproduce the same pair trades deterministically.

Replay Mode — /replay

Re-runs a recorded paper session through the paper onTick() handler. Useful for tuning straddle parameters (squeeze ratio, target/stop %, VIX cheap threshold) against real recorded ticks after market hours.

⚠ 11. Known Gaps & Roadmap

  • Crash-recovery gap. src/utils/positionPersist.js does not yet handle Straddle pairs. The other strategies (Swing / Scalp / PA) write .active_*_position.json on entry and reconcile against broker positions on boot; Straddle does not. If a process crash happens with an open Straddle Live pair, both legs will be orphaned at the broker and not automatically squared off. Mitigation: add saveStraddlePosition() / loadStraddlePosition() / clearStraddlePosition() helpers that persist both legs together (one JSON file with { ce: {...}, pe: {...}, netDebit, entryTime }), and wire them into straddlePaper.js + straddleLive.js + app.js boot reconciliation. Until then, prefer Paper or DRY-RUN for unattended Straddle sessions.
  • Auto-unwind on partial fill not implemented. When the CE fills but PE fails (or vice versa), the strategy logs an alert but leaves you with a one-sided position. An optional auto-unwind path could try to cancel/exit the filled leg if the second leg fails — trade-off is potential double-slippage during a fast move. Decision deferred to post-data-collection.
  • Telegram per-strategy toggles not wired. Straddle session-start / entry / exit / signal alerts currently fire under the master TG_ENABLED gate only. There are no TG_STRADDLE_* toggles in src/utils/notify.js. Adding them mirrors the pattern used for Swing / Scalp / PA.
  • STRONG vs MARGINAL classification is implicit. The VIX STRONG_ONLY gate references signal strength, but the strategy does not yet attach an explicit STRONG/MARGINAL label to each entry record (other strategies do). Add the classification field to the entry log so post-window analysis can attribute P&L by strength bucket.
  • No per-side leg-specific exit logic. By design (see §6), exits fire only on the combined premium. If you want to add a per-leg exit (e.g. "if PE has lost 80%, close it and let CE run as a directional bet"), it's an additive change in src/strategies/straddle_volatility.js. The current design intentionally avoids this complexity.
Tuning posture: Straddle is currently in the active paper-trade data-collection window (through ∼2026-06-02). During this window: additive logging only, no strategy tuning, no backtest-focused changes. Any of the above gaps should be addressed only as additive features (don't touch existing entry / fill / exit math). See project_paper_trade_data_collection.md for the rationale.
