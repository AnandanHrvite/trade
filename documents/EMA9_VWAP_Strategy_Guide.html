⚙ 1. Strategy Overview

EMA 9 + VWAP mirrors a TradingView setup traded by eye: the 9-period EMA (on the 5-minute close) crossing a session-anchored VWAP with ±σ Standard-Deviation bands (price source HLC3). It is a 5-minute, intraday, single-position strategy — one open CE or PE at a time, single leg, no paired hedge. Data comes off the shared Fyers spot feed (the same NIFTY50 tick stream every strategy in this suite subscribes to via socketManager); when live, orders route to Zerodha through the paper-wrapping harness (Live = Paper).

The thesis is momentum-with-value: the VWAP±1σ band is a statistical envelope around the session's average price. When the fast EMA punches through the top band, price is committing to a directional move that has already stretched a full standard deviation above the mean — a CE cue. A push below the bottom band is the mirror PE cue. The position is then held to a pure signal exit (the EMA re-crossing back inside the band) rather than a fixed stop or target, so a clean trend runs to its natural end.

VWAP caveat (same as ORB): the NIFTY spot index carries no real per-bar volume in the Fyers feed, so this VWAP degenerates to a session-anchored TWAP±σ (equal / tick-count-weighted HLC3, with the σ band computed from the TWAP variance). The band math and the EMA9 cross are exact; the absolute VWAP value tracks TradingView's shape closely but is not tick-identical.

📈 2. Indicators — EMA 9 + VWAP ±σ Bands

Two indicators, both computed on the closed 5-minute candle series. The EMA is a standard exponential moving average from the technicalindicators package; the VWAP and its σ bands are computed in-strategy, session-anchored and reset every trading day.

Exponential Moving Average

A EMA9VWAP_EMA_PERIOD-length EMA (default 9) on the candle close. This is the line that does the crossing. The strategy compares the EMA at the last two closed candles (ema9Now vs ema9Prev) against the band lines at those same candles to detect a series crossover — not a single-point “is it above / below” test.

Session-Anchored VWAP with ±σ Bands

Anchored at EMA9VWAP_VWAP_SESSION_START (default 09:15 IST) and reset each trading day, the VWAP accumulates the typical price HLC3 = (high + low + close) / 3 across every in-session candle. The bands are placed at ± the multiplier times the standard deviation of that HLC3 series:

VWAP        = Σ(HLC3) / N            (equal-weight TWAP on the volumeless index)
σ           = sqrt( Σ(HLC3²)/N − VWAP² )
Top line    = VWAP + (EMA9VWAP_BAND_MULT × σ)
Bottom line = VWAP − (EMA9VWAP_BAND_MULT × σ)
VariableDefaultEffect
EMA9VWAP_EMA_PERIOD9EMA length crossed against the band
EMA9VWAP_BAND_MULT1σ multiplier. 1 = ±1σ (the TradingView default). 0 collapses the band to the plain VWAP line (EMA9-vs-VWAP crossover).
EMA9VWAP_VWAP_SESSION_START09:15Session anchor. VWAP + σ accumulate from here and reset daily.
Candles build from 09:15, trading starts later. The VWAP anchors at the open so the band is fully warmed by the time the entry window opens (10:30 by default). If real per-bar volume were ever present, the code auto-switches to a true volume-weighted VWAP and volume-weighted variance — but on the spot index it always takes the equal-weight TWAP path.

▶ 3. Entry Logic — The Cross

Entries are evaluated on candle close (“wait for the timeframe close”). A single crossover fires the trade — there is no multi-gate pipeline like ORB. Every signal is classified STRONG (the strategy has no MARGINAL tier).

CE
EMA9 crosses ABOVE the top line
The fast EMA was at or below the VWAP+σ band on the prior candle and closes above it on this one — momentum breaking out above the upper envelope.
ema9Prev ≤ upperPrev  AND  ema9Now > upperNow
PE
EMA9 crosses BELOW the bottom line
Mirror image — the EMA was at or above the VWAP−σ band and closes below it, breaking down through the lower envelope.
ema9Prev ≥ lowerPrev  AND  ema9Now < lowerNow

Entry Window

New entries are only taken inside the intraday window. Candles are built from the 09:15 open (so the VWAP band and EMA are warm), but the strategy waits until the window opens before it will act on a cross.

VariableDefaultDescription
EMA9VWAP_ENTRY_START10:30Earliest new entry (IST). Skips the noisy first hour.
EMA9VWAP_ENTRY_END14:30Latest new entry (IST). A position opened before this can still trail past it — only new entries stop here.

When Does It Fire?

By default both EMA9VWAP_INTRACANDLE_ENTRY and EMA9VWAP_CONFIRM_CANDLE_ENABLED are OFF, so the entry fills on the cross candle's close — no waiting for a confirmation candle, and no acting on an intra-candle touch before the bar has closed.

VariableDefaultBehaviour when ON
EMA9VWAP_INTRACANDLE_ENTRYfalseAct on the cross the instant it happens intra-candle, without waiting for the bar to close.
EMA9VWAP_CONFIRM_CANDLE_ENABLEDfalseRequire an extra confirmation candle in the signal direction before entering.

Entry-Reason Fingerprint

The log line surfaces the EMA value, the band line it crossed, and the VWAP/σ that defined it:

EMA9 24312.5 crossed ABOVE VWAP top 24298.1 (VWAP 24270.4 +1σ)   [STRONG]

This EMA9-crosses-VWAP±σ fingerprint is what disambiguates an EMA9+VWAP trade from EMA_RSI_ST / BB_RSI / PA / ORB in a mixed session log — see the signal-signatures memory note.

📍 4. Exit Logic — Precedence Order

The position opens with stopLoss = null — there is no default stop-loss, target, or trailing stop. It is held in full until one of the exits below fires. On each closed candle they are evaluated in this order; the first match squares off the whole position.

1
2-candle reversal engulf  default ON
Bail if the just-closed candle reverses HARD against the position. For a CE: a bearish candle (close < open) that closes below both of the previous 2 candles' lows. For a PE: a bullish candle that closes above both of the previous 2 candles' highs. Rolling reference (each closed candle measured against its own prior 2). Candle-close only.
EMA9VWAP_REVERSAL_EXIT_ENABLED = true
2
Pure signal exit — EMA9 re-crosses back inside the band
The core exit. A CE exits when the EMA re-crosses back below the top line (VWAP+σ); a PE exits when it re-crosses back above the bottom line (VWAP−σ). Momentum has returned inside the statistical envelope — the reason to be in the trade is gone.
CE: exitCE (EMA9 < upper)  |  PE: exitPE (EMA9 > lower)
3
Exit before day close
Any position still open at EMA9VWAP_EOD_EXIT_TIME (default 15:15 IST) is squared off ahead of the engine's auto-stop. A trailing trade that survived the entry window closes here.
time ≥ EMA9VWAP_EOD_EXIT_TIME (15:15)
4
EOD square-off + engine stop
Final backstop — at EMA9VWAP_STOP_TIME (default 15:30, resolved via TRADE_STOP_TIME) any open position is squared off and the engine auto-stops for the day, releasing its socket callback.
time ≥ EMA9VWAP_STOP_TIME (15:30)
“Pure signal exit” is not the only fast exit. With the reversal engulf ON by default, a hard reversal candle can square the trade off before the EMA re-crosses the band — it sits ahead of the signal exit in precedence. Turn EMA9VWAP_REVERSAL_EXIT_ENABLED=false to hold purely to the signal / EOD exit.

🚫 5. Optional Stops (Off by Default)

None of the following are active out of the box — the strategy ships as a pure signal-exit engine. They are catastrophe / experimentation levers you can switch on via Settings. All are additive on top of the four exits in §4.

StopVariableDefaultEffect when enabled
Option-premium stop EMA9VWAP_OPT_STOP_PCT 0 Cut the trade when the option LTP falls this fraction below entry premium. 0 = off.
Spot-points stop EMA9VWAP_STOP_LOSS_PTS 0 Hard spot-move stop in points against the position. 0 = off.
Candle-trail stop EMA9VWAP_CANDLE_TRAIL_ENABLED / _BARS false / 3 N-bar structural trailing stop — CE trails to the N-bar low, PE to the N-bar high, tighten-only. Enforced intra-candle once set.
Negative-candle cut EMA9VWAP_NEG_CANDLE_LIMIT 0 Square off a trade that is still in the red after N candles held (winners keep riding). 0 = off. An in-code comment wrongly says “default 2” — the resolved default is 0.
Legacy time-stop EMA9VWAP_SL_MODE ema Set to candle to re-enable the shared candles-held time-stop guard. ema (default) = pure signal exit, the time-stop stays inert.
Why default-off? The strategy's edge is holding a clean trend to the signal exit. A tight premium / points stop would chop the winners short; these levers exist for regime experimentation and live-risk insurance, not as part of the core thesis.

🛡 6. Guards & VIX Filter

India VIX Regime Filter — GLOBAL keys, not per-mode

Unlike ORB / BB_RSI / PA (each of which has its own *_VIX_* block), EMA9+VWAP has no per-mode VIX keys. Its VIX check calls the shared filter defaulting to the EMA_RSI_ST/global “swing” mode, so it reads the global VIX_* settings.

VariableDefaultEffect
VIX_FILTER_ENABLEDtrueOn unless explicitly false. When on, blocks entries when VIX is out of band.
VIX_MAX_ENTRY20Above this VIX, entries are blocked entirely — premiums pumped, R:R deteriorates.
VIX_STRONG_ONLY16Between this and VIX_MAX_ENTRY, only STRONG signals fire. Every EMA9+VWAP signal is STRONG, so in practice this tier does not filter it out.
VIX is not independently tunable for this strategy. Because it shares the EMA_RSI_ST/global thresholds, changing VIX_MAX_ENTRY moves the gate for EMA_RSI_ST too. There are no EMA9VWAP_VIX_* keys to override it in isolation.

Liquidity Guards (Live-Only)

The shared OI-buildup and bid-ask spread guards run under the ema9vwap mode key, but only on the live path and only when explicitly enabled. Paper and backtest do not apply them.

Daily-count gate (pre-filter). Before any signal is acted on, the strategy checks the daily trade cap and the daily-loss kill-switch (§7). Once either trips, further candles are skipped for the session.

🛑 7. Risk, Cooldowns & Circuit Breakers

LayerVariableDefaultEffect
Daily trade capEMA9VWAP_MAX_DAILY_TRADES20After N entries, no more for the session. Falls back to global MAX_DAILY_TRADES if unset.
Daily loss kill-switchEMA9VWAP_MAX_DAILY_LOSS5000 ₹Realised loss ≥ threshold halts entries for the day. Falls back to global MAX_DAILY_LOSS.
Consecutive-loss breaker—auto3 straight losses → 5-minute pause (4 candles). A 15-minute variant kills the strategy for the day. A profitable trade clears any remaining pause.

Same-side SL cooldownEMA9VWAP_SL_PAUSE_CANDLES3After an SL hit, block a same-side re-entry for N candles. Inert unless an optional stop (§5) is enabled — the pure signal exit is not an SL.
Opposite-side flip cooldownEMA9VWAP_OPPOSITE_SIDE_COOLDOWN_ENABLED / _CANDLEStrue / 3After a signal-cross OR reversal-engulf exit, block the flip side for N candles — prevents whipsaw flips on chop. Skipped for EOD / day-close / manual / opposite-signal exits.
Why the opposite-side cooldown matters here. With the reversal engulf ON, a CE can be closed on a hard bearish candle — which is exactly the kind of bar that would also print a PE cross. The 3-candle flip cooldown stops the engine from instantly reversing into that fresh loss, letting the chop pass before it re-engages.

🧮 8. Worked Example

A textbook CE trade on a trending morning, all defaults (band mult 1, entry window 10:30–14:30, reversal exit ON):

10:20  VWAP = 24270,  σ = 28   →  top line = 24298, bottom = 24242
       EMA9 = 24291  (below the top line — no cross)

10:35  Candle CLOSES. EMA9 ticks up to 24313; top line now 24301.
       ema9Prev (24296) ≤ upperPrev (24299)  AND  ema9Now (24313) > upperNow (24301)
       →  CE ENTRY at the candle close. Buy 1 ATM CE. stopLoss = null (no SL/target).
       Log: "EMA9 24313 crossed ABOVE VWAP top 24301 (VWAP 24274 +1σ) [STRONG]"

11:00–12:40  Price trends up. EMA9 stays above the top line every close.
       Position simply HELD — no trail, no target to hit.

Three ways this trade can end (whichever comes first):
  (a) A bearish 5-min candle closes below BOTH of the prior 2 candles' lows
      → 2-candle reversal exit, squared off at that candle's close.
  (b) EMA9 re-crosses back BELOW the top line on a candle close
      → pure signal exit ("EMA9 re-entered VWAP top band").
  (c) Neither happens — position squared off at 15:15 (exit-before-close),
      or the 15:30 EOD backstop.

After the exit, the opposite (PE) side is blocked for 3 candles by the
flip cooldown, so a whipsaw bearish poke can't instantly flip the trade.
The whole edge in one line: enter on the EMA punching a full standard deviation through the band, then get out of the way and let the trend run — capped only by a hard reversal candle, the EMA folding back inside the band, or the clock.

⚙ 9. Operating Modes & Live Gating

Paper Mode — /ema9vwap-paper

Real-time Fyers tick feed via socketManager; simulated fills (no broker round-trip). This is the canonical decision/fill/exit logic — the source of truth. Sessions persist to ~/trading-data/ema9vwap_paper_trades.json; a per-day JSONL audit lands at ~/trading-data/trades/ema9vwap_paper_trades_YYYY-MM-DD.jsonl, seeded with a settings snapshot at session start and re-snapshotted on every Settings save during the session. When TICK_RECORDER_ENABLED=true (default) every spot/option/VIX tick is recorded for Replay.

Live Mode — /ema9vwap-live (Live = Paper)

The live route runs the same paper engine and wraps it in the harness, placing real Zerodha orders on entry/exit. It is double-gated: both EMA9VWAP_LIVE_ENABLED=true AND LIVE_HARNESS_DRY_RUN=false are required for a real order. A per-mode EMA9VWAP_LIVE_DRY_RUN=true holds this strategy in dry-run (log the broker call, place nothing) even when the global harness is armed — letting you take other strategies live while EMA9+VWAP stays simulated. Flip DRY-RUN off only after Live and Paper decisions match on a recorded session via /replay.

Backtest Mode — /ema9vwap-backtest

A dedicated candle-loop engine (ema9vwapBacktestEngine.js) that replays historical 5-minute candles and mirrors the paper decisions exactly — reversal engulf, pure signal exit, EOD, opposite-side cooldown, VIX and daily-loss guards were all ported so the backtest reproduces paper behaviour rather than approximating it.

Replay Mode — /replay

Re-runs a recorded paper session through the paper onTick() handler. Snapshot applies the recorded session-start settings (deterministic A/A reproduction); Current Settings applies the live process.env (A/B for after-hours tuning against real ticks). Outputs land in ~/trading-data/_replay_trades/ (snapshot) or _replay_trades_sim/ (current settings), kept separate from the canonical paper logs.

⚠ 11. Notes & Known Gaps

  • New strategy, no tuning history. Added 2026-06-28 with no prior analysis cohort. Collect clean live/paper sessions before tuning any threshold — there is no validated baseline yet.
  • Reversal engulf can double up with the signal exit. Both are ON in spirit by default; on choppy days the 2-candle reversal may exit eagerly and, combined with the flip cooldown, sit the strategy out of a subsequent re-entry. Watch for over-eager exits before deciding whether to keep EMA9VWAP_REVERSAL_EXIT_ENABLED on.
  • VIX is not independently tunable. The gate shares EMA_RSI_ST/global VIX_* thresholds — there is no EMA9VWAP_VIX_* block, so you cannot loosen/tighten VIX for this strategy in isolation.
  • VWAP is a TWAP on the spot index. No real volume → the VWAP degenerates to session TWAP±σ (same as ORB). Band math and the EMA cross are exact; absolute VWAP tracks TradingView's shape but is not tick-identical. Don't expect the on-screen VWAP number to match TV to the point.
  • In-code comment drift. The negative-candle-cut comment says “default 2”, but the resolved default of EMA9VWAP_NEG_CANDLE_LIMIT is 0 (off). Trust the code default, not the comment.
Tuning posture: the paper-data-collection no-tuning window was lifted on 2026-05-27, so tuning is in scope — but this strategy has no clean post-launch cohort yet. Gather sessions first, then use /replay Current-Settings mode to A/B named hypotheses against real recorded ticks before changing defaults.
